Hi, I'm Dev. I work at Google. My interests are distributed system infrastructure, performance monitoring and large-scale machine learning platforms. To keep it short and simple, I'm a Software Craftsmith ... half Java Junkie, half C++ worshipper and (sic:) one-third Pythonista with a dash of Ruby.

Bio

Dev is a Software Engineer at Google, based out of Mountain View, California. He works on system scalability and performance improvements for Google Shopping Ads serving infrastructure. He earned his MS degree in Computer Science in 2015 from the CS Dept. at the University of California, Santa Barbara. At UC Santa Barbara, he worked at the RACE Lab advised by Prof. Chandra Krintz and Prof. Rich Wolski. on StochSS. StochSS was a NIH funded project whose aim is to provide an integrated development environment (IDE) for running discrete stochastic simulations of biochemical networks and population systems in cloud computing environments. Dev's chief contribution was a generic cloud computing framework for configuring virtual machines and deploying arbitrary scientific simulation programs in the cloud (public or private) by wrapping the source code as a web service. Dev also has been a software engineering intern AppFolio, a SaaS startup, focused on property management vertical. At Appfolio, he worked on their data analytics team for property rental price prediction.

Prior to Google and UCSB, he worked at McAfee on enterprise data protection products, which included transparent enforceable encryption of files and removable media.
